How to Read the Relative Rotation Graph

The RRG (Relative Rotation Graph) plots each sector's relative strength (RS-Ratio, X-axis) against its momentum (RS-Momentum, Y-axis) compared to the NIFTY 50 benchmark. The center (100,100) represents parity with the benchmark. Sectors rotate clockwise through the four quadrants over time — this rotation reflects the natural cycle of sector leadership.

Improving (Top-Left)

RS-Ratio < 100, RS-Momentum > 100 — Sector still underperforms but momentum is turning up. Early rotation signal. Watch for breakout into Leading.

Leading (Top-Right)

RS-Ratio > 100, RS-Momentum > 100 — Sector is outperforming and acceleration is positive. The strongest position. Best sectors for momentum trades.

Lagging (Bottom-Left)

RS-Ratio < 100, RS-Momentum < 100 — Sector underperforms with declining momentum. Weakest position. Avoid until momentum turns. Watch for rotation into Improving.

Weakening (Bottom-Right)

RS-Ratio > 100, RS-Momentum < 100 — Sector still outperforms but momentum is fading. Leadership is ending. Consider booking profits.

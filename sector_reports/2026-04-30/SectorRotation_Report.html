How to Read the Relative Rotation Graph

The RRG plots each sector's relative strength (RS-Ratio, X-axis) against its momentum (RS-Momentum, Y-axis) compared to the NIFTY 50 benchmark. The center (100,100) represents parity. Sectors rotate clockwise through the four quadrants — this rotation reflects the natural cycle of sector leadership.

Improving (Top-Left)

RS-Ratio < 100, RS-Momentum > 100 — Sector still underperforms but momentum is turning up. Early rotation signal.

Leading (Top-Right)

RS-Ratio > 100, RS-Momentum > 100 — Sector is outperforming and acceleration is positive. Strongest position.

Lagging (Bottom-Left)

RS-Ratio < 100, RS-Momentum < 100 — Sector underperforms with declining momentum. Weakest position.

Weakening (Bottom-Right)

RS-Ratio > 100, RS-Momentum < 100 — Sector still outperforms but momentum is fading. Consider booking profits.
